Guide instead of instructing

Teaching Perspective

The intention of the New School of Mathematics is simple: to give the minimum of instructions to the student so that she is the one who finds the way. We do not want to be a traditional school where the student first listens, and then repeats specific exercises.

We want to do it the other way around:

We want to listen to the student first, what problem she wants to solve. Then we ask what ideas he has and from there we encourage him to explore those that we as teachers or guides we know they can fix the problem. The intention is for the student to work and think for herself, and is not limited to copying and repeating mechanically as is currently the case in almost all schools of Catalonia.

  • Secondary Classes (ESO)

    Understand the following content, in general:

    math

    • Sense of number and operations
    • Language and algebraic calculus
    • Patterns, relationships, and functions
    • Change analysis and function types
    • Spatial sense and representation of three-dimensional figures
    • Metric relations and calculation of measures in figures
    • Statistics
    • Measurement and probability

    Physics and Chemistry

    • Mechanical and electromagnetic wave model. Light ray model.
    • Chemical change model.
    • Atomic-molecular model, chemical bond, intermolecular forces. Model structure of substances.
    • Energy, forces and movements
    • Measurement and probability
    • Industrial technological systems. Simple and complex machines
    • Technological objects based on mechanics, electricity, electronics and pneumatics
    • Electric current and effects. Electricity generation.

  • High School Classes

    Includes the following content, in general:

    math

    • Arithmetic and algebra (number theory, decimals, polynomials, progressions)
    • Geometry (circle, trigonometry, vectors)
    • Analysis (functions, exchange rates, derivatives)
    • Statistics (probability, relationships between variables, combinations)

    physics

    • Images (light ray model, light behavior, light spectrum, polarization)
    • Motion (Cartesian plane, uniform motion, uniform circular, parabola)
    • Mechanics (Force, friction, tension, ppi action Newton's reaction, vector system, shocks, conservation of momentum)
    • Energy (work and heat, transfer, in. kinetics, gravitational potential, transformation, measurement, power, dissipation)
    • Electricity (direct / alternating current, current, potential, resistance, Ohm's law, transferred power, electromotive force, series or parallel resistors, electrical circuit, sensors)

    chemistry

    • atomic-molecular models (molecular structure, Lavoisier nomenclature and formulation, atomic and molecular mass, mol, Avogadro's constant, periodic table)
    • Gases and liquids (diff. between fact, hypothesis, experiment and law, pressure-volume relationship, temperature, gas kinematics, surface tension, solutions, partial pressure)
    • Bohr atomic model (subatomic particles, atomic number, light wave-particle duality, Schrödinger model)
    • bonds (Lewis atomic model, covalent bond, molecular bond, atomic bond, intra-molecular forces, dipole-dipole forces, hydrogen bonding, crystals)
    • organic chemistry (properties, isomers, formulation and nomenclature, good fuel, biofuels, drug synthesis)
    • chemical reactions (molecular interaction, acids and bases, pH, precipitation, redox, polymers, oxidation)

  • University Classes

    Any topic related to

    math :

    • Practical algebra (number theory, bases, polynomials, progressions)
    • Advanced algebra (Matrix diagonalization, characteristic polynomial, projections, orthonormal bases)
    • Euclidean geometry (Cartesian coordinates, vectors, calculation of distances between planes and lines, base changes, base change matrices, dual space)
    • Affine Geometry (parallel lines, axioms, Pappus's law, Discharges, affine transformation, affine space, projective)
    • Calculating functions (functions, exchange rates, derivatives, tangents)
    • Integral calculus (simple and complex integrals, in 1D, in 2D, in 3D)
    • Pure calculus (lim sup, lim inf, def in epsilon delta, fundamental calculus theorem)
    • Practical probability (measure, Newton's binomial, random variable, combinatorial)
    • Pure probability (measure theory, probability space, independent / dependent random variables, Lebesgue measure, Fatou lemma, theorem of the Lebesgue-dominated convergence)
    • Function distribution statistics (probability distributions, Gaussian distribution, Poisson, Dirach, uniform, logistics, beta, gamma, exponential, Cauchy)
    • Prediction and hypothesis statistics (hypothesis tests, linear regression, adjusted values, error distribution as a random variable, error measurement, likelihood, parametric and nonparametric regressions)
    • 1-variable differential equations (linear, homogeneous, constant / variable coefficients, first-order linear ED, flow charts, source points and well points, vector field, trajectory approx. of particles)
    • Computational algebra (QR factorization, fact. LU, decomposition into eigenvalues, Gaussian method, solution of linear systems, conditioning and stability of a problem, stability of an algorithm, iterative methods)
